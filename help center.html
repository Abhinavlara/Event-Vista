Frequently Asked Questions

You can reset your password by navigating to the Reset Password page from the Help Center menu.

Payment failures can occur due to bank restrictions or insufficient balance. Visit the Payment Issues page for full troubleshooting steps.

You can use the contact form above or use live chat available in the support dashboard.

Reset Password

If you have forgotten your password or are unable to log in, you can reset it easily using the "Forgot Password" option on the login page. You will receive an email or OTP on your registered phone to verify your identity. Follow the link or enter the OTP to create a new password. Make sure to choose a strong password to keep your account secure.

Payment Issues

Payment may fail due to reasons such as insufficient balance, expired card, network issues, or bank restrictions. Ensure that your card or UPI is active, and your bank has not blocked online transactions. If the amount is deducted but not reflected, it will be refunded within 3–5 business days. Visit your transaction history page for more details.

Delete Account

If you wish to permanently delete your account, navigate to Settings → Privacy → Delete Account. You will be required to verify your identity using an OTP or email link. Once confirmed, your account and all associated data will be permanently removed and cannot be recovered. Download your data before proceeding.

Change Phone Number

You can update your phone number in your profile settings. After entering the new number, you will receive a verification OTP. Once verified, your new number will be attached to your account. All notifications, alerts, and security messages will be sent to this updated number.

Two-Step Verification

Two-step verification adds an extra layer of security to your account by requiring both your password and a verification code during login. You can enable this feature from the Security Settings. Your verification code may be delivered through SMS, email, or an authenticator app depending on your preference.
